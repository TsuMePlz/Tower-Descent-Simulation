Introduction

Welcome to the official player guide for Tower Descent Simulation, an immersive roguelike experience set in the My Hero Academia universe. In this challenging training exercise, you will guide the students of U.A. High School Class 1-A through 20 increasingly difficult zones as they descend into the depths of an underground facility.

This guide has been designed to help both new players learn the fundamentals and experienced players master advanced strategies. Whether you are attempting your first run or going for a perfect clear with zero captures, you will find valuable information within these pages.

What is Tower Descent Simulation?

Tower Descent Simulation is a turn-based roguelike game where strategic decision-making and resource management are key to success. You will deploy students one at a time into procedurally generated zones, engaging in tactical combat, exploring for valuable resources, and facing powerful boss enemies.

üéØ Core Gameplay Loop
  1. Select a student from Class 1-A
  2. Navigate through randomly generated zone rooms
  3. Search points of interest for items and rescued students
  4. Engage enemies in turn-based tactical combat
  5. Defeat the zone boss to progress deeper
  6. Repeat for 20 zones until facing the final boss

Key Features

  • 21 Playable Characters - Each Class 1-A student has unique abilities, stats, and personal skills
  • Dual Skill System - Global skills benefit everyone, personal skills provide powerful bonuses but are lost if captured
  • Environmental Effects - Seven zone types with different effects on character performance
  • Team-Up Attacks - 28 devastating combination attacks between Level 10+ partners
  • Party Buff Skills - Support characters can unlock abilities that benefit the entire team
  • Persistent Progression - Global skills and boss damage carry between attempts
  • Adaptive Final Boss - All For One grows stronger based on how many students you lost
üí° First Time Players

If this is your first time playing, we recommend starting with the Game Controls section to familiarize yourself with the interface, then reading Combat Mechanics and Character Roles before beginning your first run. The early game tips in the strategy section will help you avoid common mistakes.

Game Controls

Tower Descent Simulation uses a text-based command interface. All actions are performed by typing commands and pressing Enter.

Basic Navigation

When exploring zones or navigating menus, you will see numbered options. Simply type the number corresponding to your choice and press Enter.

Example: Character Selection
1. Izuku Midoriya
2. Katsuki Bakugo
3. Shoto Todoroki

Choose your student: 1

Combat Commands

During combat, you will have multiple action options:

  • 1 - Attack: Perform a basic attack using your current attack stat
  • 2 - Quirk: Use a powerful quirk ability that costs energy
  • 3 - Item: Use an item from your shared inventory
  • 4 - Skills: Access the skill tree menu mid-combat
  • 5 - Team-Up: Execute a devastating team-up attack (requires Level 10+ partner)
  • 6 - Plus Ultra: Full HP and Energy recovery (once per zone, requires all skills maxed)
‚ö†Ô∏è Important

The Team-Up and Plus Ultra options only appear when their requirements are met. The menu will dynamically adjust based on what is available to you.

Exploration Commands

While exploring zone rooms, you will have these options:

  • Move to Adjacent Room: Type the number of the room to move there
  • Search POI: Investigate a point of interest in your current room
  • Rest: Restore 15 energy (once per room)
  • Enter Boss Room: Face the zone boss when at the boss room

Special Commands

These commands can be used at any time:

  • froppenheimer: Opens the debug menu for testing purposes
  • back: Return to previous menu (context-dependent)

Auto-Save System

The game automatically saves your progress after completing each zone. Your save is stored in your browser's local storage and persists between sessions.

üíæ Save Management
  • Game auto-saves after each zone clear
  • Use "Continue" button on title screen to resume
  • Starting a new game will overwrite your current save
  • Save data includes all character progress and global skills

Combat Mechanics

Combat in Tower Descent Simulation is turn-based and tactical. Understanding the combat system is essential for success, especially in later zones and boss fights.

Combat Flow

  1. You select an action for your character
  2. Your action is executed (damage dealt, ability used, etc)
  3. If enemy survives, they attack back
  4. Turn ends, cycle repeats until combat resolves

Damage Calculation

Damage in combat is calculated using this formula:

Damage Formula

Actual Damage = max(1, Base Damage - Target Defense)

Where:

  • Base Damage = Attack stat + random variance (-3 to +5)
  • Target Defense = Defender's defense stat
  • Minimum Damage = 1 (attacks always deal at least 1 damage)

Attack Types

Basic Attack: Uses your attack stat directly with slight random variance. No energy cost. Good for conserving energy against weak enemies.

Quirk Abilities: Powerful attacks with fixed high damage values. Cost energy to use. Each character has 2-3 unique quirk abilities with different costs and damage outputs.

Example Quirks Damage Energy Cost Best Used When Detroit Smash (Midoriya) 20 + ATK 12 High single-target damage Full Cowling (Midoriya) 40 + ATK 30 Boss fights, emergencies AP Shot (Bakugo) 18 + ATK 10 Efficient damage option

Many characters have passive abilities that modify combat:

  • Katsuki Bakugo - Ambush Master: +30% damage on first strike
  • Shoto Todoroki - Versatile Combat: +5% to all combat stats

‚öîÔ∏è Combat Strategy

Early Combat: Use basic attacks to conserve energy. Save quirk abilities for when you need burst damage or are low on HP.

Mid Combat: Monitor enemy HP. If they are close to death, a basic attack may finish them and save energy.

Boss Combat: Do not hold back. Use your most powerful abilities. Remember that boss HP persists, so damaging them even if you lose helps future attempts.

Special Combat Mechanics

Stun System

When Denki Kaminari hits an enemy, there is a 33% chance to stun them. Stunned enemies skip their next turn completely, giving you a free action.

Acid Counter

When Mina Ashido has her Acid Veil skill unlocked, enemies take recoil damage when they attack anyone. This can finish off low-HP enemies.

Last Stand Mechanics

Both Ojiro and Midoriya have powerful abilities that activate when their HP drops to 25% or below. Ojiro deals double damage, while Midoriya takes half damage. Use this to turn the tide of difficult battles.

‚ö†Ô∏è Combat Death

If a character's HP reaches 0, they are captured and removed from the roster for the rest of the run. Their personal skills are reset. The only way to recover them is to find them during POI searches in later zones.

Exploration & Navigation

Each zone consists of multiple interconnected rooms. Your goal is to navigate to the boss room while managing resources and searching for valuable items.

Zone Structure

Zones are procedurally generated with the following elements:

  • Starting Room: Where you begin each zone
  • Standard Rooms: May contain enemies, POI, or be empty
  • Boss Room: Contains the zone boss (marked with ‚ö†Ô∏è)
  • Secret Passages: Hidden connections between rooms

Points of Interest (POI)

POI are searchable locations within rooms. They can contain:

  • Items (45% base chance) - Health Potions, Energy Drinks, equipment
  • Enemies (25% base chance) - Combat encounters
  • Rescued Students (5% base chance) - Recover captured allies
  • Civilians (5% base chance) - Rescue for rewards
  • Secret Passages (10% base chance) - Shortcuts through zones
  • Lucky Bags (5% chance for Yaoyorozu only) - Massive rewards
üîç Scout Characters Excel Here

Characters like Toru Hagakure, Tenya Iida, and Hanta Sero have passive abilities that increase POI discovery rates. Use them to find rescued students and secret passages more frequently.

Rest System

You can rest once per room to restore 15 energy. Use this strategically before boss fights or after heavy combat.

Passive Healing

Characters not currently deployed heal passively as you progress:

Healing Formula

HP per Room = 1 + (Vitality Global Skill / 15)

With Ochaco Uraraka deployed: +2 additional HP per room

When upgrading global skills, consider who is deployed. If Sato is your current character, upgrading Strength gives him a massive boost. If Kirishima is deployed, upgrading Defense is extremely efficient. Plan your skill point spending around your active character's specializations.

Boss Fight Strategies

Boss fights occur every 5 zones and at zone 20 (final boss). These encounters are significantly harder than regular combat.

Boss Mechanics

  • Persistent HP: Boss health carries over between attempts
  • Higher Stats: Bosses have 2-3x the HP of regular enemies
  • Scaling Difficulty: Stats increase with zone number
  • Strategic Deployment: You can retreat and switch characters

Preparation Tips

üéØ Pre-Boss Checklist
  1. Heal Up: Use health potions to get deployed character to full HP
  2. Restore Energy: Rest in the room before boss if possible
  3. Check Inventory: Ensure you have emergency items
  4. Review Skills: Spend any available skill points
  5. Character Choice: Consider deploying a tank for safety or damage dealer to finish quickly

Multi-Attempt Strategy

Because boss HP persists, you can employ a "chip damage" strategy:

  1. First Attempt: Deploy a high-damage character (Sato, Bakugo) to deal massive damage
  2. Second Attempt: If first character falls, deploy a tank (Kirishima, Todoroki) to finish the weakened boss safely

Final Boss: All For One

The final boss at zone 20 is unique and adaptive:

‚ö†Ô∏è Adaptive Difficulty

All For One grows stronger based on captured students:

  • Base Stats: 200 HP, 20 ATK, 10 DEF
  • Per Captured Student: +50 HP, +5 ATK, +2 DEF
  • He gains the quirks of captured students (flavor text)

Perfect Run (0 captures): Much easier fight with special recognition

All For One Strategy

  1. Bring Your Best: Deploy your highest-level, most-equipped character
  2. Use Team-Ups: If you have Level 10+ partners available, use team-up attacks
  3. Plus Ultra Ready: Have at least one character with Plus Ultra unlocked
  4. Save Party Buffs: If Asui has Ribbit Recovery, she provides a safety net

Early Game Tips (Zones 1-7)

First Zone Priority

Your first zone sets the foundation for your entire run:

  1. Deploy a Balanced Character: Midoriya or Todoroki are excellent first choices
  2. Invest in Global Skills: Put your first skill points into Strength or HP
  3. Learn Combat Flow: Practice basic attack vs quirk ability timing
  4. Search Everything: POI searches give valuable items early

Skill Point Strategy

üìä Early Skill Distribution

Recommended Split (First 10 Points):

  • 4 points in Strength (permanent damage boost)
  • 3 points in Vitality (more HP for everyone)
  • 2 points in Defense (survivability)
  • 1 point in Stamina (energy pool)

Why this works: Focuses on survivability and consistent damage. Evasion becomes more valuable later when you understand combat flow.

Character Rotation

Do not over-rely on one character early on:

  • Deploy different characters to level them evenly
  • Use scouts (Hagakure, Iida) for exploration-heavy zones
  • Save your strongest for boss zones (5, 10, 15, 20)

Avoid These Mistakes

‚ùå Common Early Errors
  • Hoarding Items: Use health potions when needed, do not save for later
  • Ignoring Personal Skills: They are powerful early, just remember the risk
  • Rushing Bosses: Take time to prepare, rest, and equip properly
  • Deploying Randomly: Match character to zone type (Asui in water zones, etc)
